Adaptive Architecture Framework – Information and Consent

The purpose of the Adaptive Architecture Framework published here is to allow people to view, adapt and re-publish the information presented. We are investigating how to best present the field of Adaptive Architecture and how to draw on distributed expertise to improve that presentation. The framework was developed as part of research projects at the Mixed Reality Lab, School of Computer Science, University of Nottingham (funded by the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research council through grant EP/I031413/1 and the University of Nottingham through the Adaptive Architecture as the Interface to Personal Data project).

Viewing – no registration required

Without logging in, you can view the presented information and explore it. No registration is required and we will not collect any data about you or your actions.

Map adaptation, creation and publication – registration required

To add, adapt and re-publish work, you need to register with your email address. By registering, you agree to take part in the research project around the Adaptive Architecture Framework.

Data collection

When you register, we will collect the following data from you:

 

- Your email address

- Your password (one-way encrypted)

- The maps that you save on the system

 

When you save a map to the system, we will collect the following information:

 

- The saved map

Data analysis – Consent

When you share a map on the system (share to all, or share with a member of the research team) you consent for the map to become part of the research to be conducted. We might analyse the map and might contact you at your email address with survey questions or the request for an interview. You will have the opportunity to decline the survey and the interview and this will not affect your use of the system. You can opt out of the research project at any point by contacting the research team or by not sharing maps with the research team (not directly and not via share all).

Data access and data storage

The data collected by this application will only be directly accessible to the researchers working on this specific research project. Data collected will be held in a secure and safe manner. The data will be stored in a secure storage server with password protection at the University of Nottingham in accordance with the Data Protection Act 1998.

Data publication

Anonymised usage data may be used in academic presentations and publications, which may result in some of your data being publicly available, e.g. on the University of Nottingham open access research publication platform. In that case, it will not be possible to identify you from what has been published.

Service availability

We do not guarantee any service of any kind. Save local copies of your information frequently. The system might be unavailable without prior warning.

Sharing information

You may share adaptations of the AAF with anyone else who is registered on the system. Only share information that you do not mind being in the public domain. We do not control the behaviour of other users who might release information that you shared with them without your consent.

Inappropriate content

Do not store or publish abusive, offensive or illegal content using the system. If you come across such content, please report it to aaframework@cs.nott.ac.uk .
